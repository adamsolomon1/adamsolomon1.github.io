            <h3>Photography Project</h3>

            <p>

                In third grade, my teacher assigned me a relatively simple assignment in photography: I was to take a few letters of the alphabet and photograph them in natural objects. The passion this assignment ignited in me compelled me to expand it significantly. I chose to photograph an object for every letter of the alphabet and then to compile them as the Alphabet Book, an illustrated guide for children to learn the alphabet. My teacher was astounded by the ingenuity, skill, and passion I had demonstrated in this assignment. He lavished praise on my collection and offered me continual encouragement throughout the remainder of my time at his school to continue expressing myself in this medium. My family was equally impressed and arranged for a book-signing event at which my customers lined up fifty-deep for me to autograph their copies. I can still recall the incredible sense of achievement and gratitude I felt on this occasion. This was the catalyst for an enduring interest in photography that has seen me significantly expand my technical skill, creative vision, and artistic ability.

            </p>

            <p>

                As a result, I completed the AP Photography course with an almost-perfect College Board portfolio score. The prompts for assigned photos were designed to tax my creative vision and technical abilities to the utmost. In particular, they were conceived to learn and master the seven elements of art in unique ways. My most prized photo from this course demonstrated my mastery of these components. The sunset shot presented a tree being doused with water, being shot upwards from sprinklers. Utilizing a very fast shutter speed, I was able to transform the droplets into scintillations which look akin to stars hovering midair about the many branches. The surreal, almost otherworldly, science fiction presentation draws the viewer in as he tries to deduce what is actually being presented, from what appears to exist before his eyes.

            </p>

            <p>

                Playing with reality by transforming everyday objects through technical wizardry is something that really appeals to me about photography. I love to capture the viewer’s imagination and draw him into a dreamworld of my conception. Even the most everyday objects in nature can be transformed through digital techniques into something truly otherworldly – captivating and wondrous. Witnessing the amazement and elation of my viewers as they encounter my work is stimulating enough to continue my explorations in photography. At UT Austin I will continue to refine my abilities and approach, seeking to delight and spark the imaginations of my viewers.

            </p>
